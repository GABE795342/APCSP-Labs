Part I: Understand Vectors

  • Magnitude and Direction

  • Think of the magnitude of a vector as the length of the hypotenuse of a right triangle.
    If the x and y properties of your vector object represent the sides of the triangle, what is the magnitude in terms of x and y?

  • Think of the direction of a vector as the angle formed by the hypotenuse and one side of the triangle
    If the x and y properties of your vector object represent the sides of the triangle, what is the direction in terms of x and y?

Part II: Vector Addition and Subtraction

  • To add vectors, we simple add their x properties and y properties

  • To subtract vectors, we simple add the opposite of their x properties and y properties

Part III: Using vector Subtraction

  • We can use vector subtraction to get a vector that points from the head of one vector to the head of another. We will use this to create a steering force.

    // p5.Vector.sub()  returns a vector
var steeringForce = p5.Vector.sub(this.loc, other.loc); 
steeringForce.normalize();  //  changes the magnitud to 1
steeringForce.mult(0.5);    //  scales the magnitude to 0.5
